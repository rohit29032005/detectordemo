Earthquake Safety Guide

🔧 BEFORE an Earthquake

  • 🧯 Secure heavy furniture & electronics.
  • 📦 Prepare emergency kits (water, food, flashlight).
  • 🏠 Identify safe zones under tables or corners.
  • 📱 Save emergency contacts in phone.
  • 👨‍👩‍👧‍👦 Conduct family safety drills regularly.

⚠ DURING an Earthquake

  • 🔻 Drop, Cover, and Hold On!
  • 🚪 Stay indoors; avoid windows and doors.
  • 🏞 If outside, move to an open area away from buildings.
  • 🚘 If in a car, stop safely and stay inside.
  • 🚫 Do not use elevators!

🚨 AFTER an Earthquake

  • 🩹 Check yourself and others for injuries.
  • 🌊 Watch for tsunami warnings (in coastal areas).
  • 🧯 Shut off gas and electricity if you smell leaks.
  • 📻 Listen to emergency broadcasts for updates.
  • 💬 Use text/WhatsApp to communicate.

📋 Emergency Kit Essentials

  • 🥫 Non-perishable food (3-day supply)
  • 💧 Water (1 gallon per person per day)
  • 🔦 Flashlight with extra batteries
  • 🧰 First aid kit with necessary meds
  • 📑 Important documents in waterproof bag
  • 🧥 Warm clothes, blankets, sturdy shoes
  • 📱 Power bank and emergency phone list

🚫 What NOT To Do During An Earthquake

  • ❌ Don’t run outside during shaking
  • ❌ Don’t stand under doorways
  • ❌ Don’t light candles or matches if you smell gas
  • ❌ Don’t use elevators
  • ❌ Don’t panic or scream – stay calm

🧠 Post-Earthquake Mental Health Tips

  • 💬 Talk about your experience with others
  • 🧘 Practice deep breathing or short meditations
  • 📖 Write down your thoughts in a journal
  • 🕊 Give yourself time to process emotions
  • 📞 Seek professional help if symptoms persist

🤔 Myth vs Fact: Earthquake Safety

  • Myth: Earthquakes only happen in the morning.
    Fact: Earthquakes can occur at any time of day or night.
  • Myth: Doorways are safest.
    Fact: Tables or sturdy furniture offer better protection.
  • Myth: Animals can predict quakes.
    Fact: No scientific evidence supports this consistently.
  • Myth: Small tremors prevent big ones.
    Fact: Small quakes don’t reduce big quake risk.

📍 Step-by-Step Earthquake Evacuation Plan

  • 1️⃣ Take cover and wait until shaking stops
  • 2️⃣ Grab emergency kit if it's safe to do so
  • 3️⃣ Exit building using stairs, not elevators
  • 4️⃣ Head to a designated safe open area
  • 5️⃣ Tune into emergency alerts and updates
